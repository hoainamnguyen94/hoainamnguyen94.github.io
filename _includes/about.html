About

I am currently a third-year PhD student in Statistics at Rice University. I often describe myself as "50% statistician, 30% mathematician and 20% computer scientist". Part of my research focuses on the use of statistical/mathematical models (e.g. branching process) to model population dynamics in biology. One example is the progression of cancer cells in malignant tumors. I am also interested in the combination of modern statistical and computational tools to tackle "big data" problems in biomedical science. For example, I am developing a Bayesian semi-parametric joint model, capable of modeling multiple primary cancers as well as competing risks from multiple cancer types, for accurate personalized cancer risk prediction. Simplified versions of my model are being experimented on real patients at MD Anderson Cancer Center.
