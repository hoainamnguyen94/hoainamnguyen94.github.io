About

I am currently a third-year PhD student in Statistics at Rice University. I often describe myself as "50% statistician, 30% mathematician and 20% computer scientist". Part of my research focuses on the use of statistical/mathematical models (e.g. branching process) to model population dynamics in biology. One example is the progression of cancer cells in malignant tumors. I am also interested in the combination of modern statistical and computational tools to tackle "big data" problems in biomedical science. For example, I am developing a Bayesian semiparametric joint model, capable of modeling multiple primary cancers as well as competing risks from multiple cancer types, for accurate personalized cancer risk prediction.
